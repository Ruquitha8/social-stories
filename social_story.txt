Here is a simple, child-friendly social story for an autistic child visiting the doctor:

**Scene 1: We're Going to the Doctor**
We are going to the doctor's office today to get a check-up and make sure our body is healthy. The doctor will help us feel better if we're sick or hurt.
Suggested image description: A picture of a car driving to a doctor's office, with a smiling child in the backseat.

**Scene 2: Waiting in the Waiting Room**
When we get to the doctor's office, we will sit in the waiting room until the doctor is ready to see us. There are toys and books to play with while we wait.
Suggested image description: A picture of a waiting room with chairs, toys, and books, and a child sitting and playing quietly.

**Scene 3: Meeting the Doctor**
The doctor will call our name and we will go into a special room to talk to them. The doctor is a friendly person who wants to help us feel better.
Suggested image description: A picture of a doctor smiling and shaking hands with a child, with a friendly nurse standing nearby.

**Scene 4: Getting a Check-Up**
The doctor will listen to our heart and look in our ears and throat to make sure everything is working properly. This might feel a little weird, but it's okay and it will be over soon.
Suggested image description: A picture of a doctor listening to a child's heart with a stethoscope, with a calm and gentle expression.

**Scene 5: Getting a Shot (if needed)**
If we need a shot, the doctor will give us a special medicine to help keep us healthy. It might hurt for a second, but then it will be all done and we can get a sticker!
Suggested image description: A picture of a doctor giving a child a shot, with a calm and reassuring expression, and a sticker or band-aid on the child's arm.

**Scene 6: We're All Done!**
After the doctor is finished, we will go home and can do all our favorite things again. We might even get a special treat for being so brave!
Suggested image description: A picture of a child leaving the doctor's office with a big smile, holding a parent's hand and carrying a favorite toy or treat.
